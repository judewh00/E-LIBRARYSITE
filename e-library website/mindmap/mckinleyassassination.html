Gitlow V. New York (1923-25)

Gitlow was arrested under New York's Criminal Anarchy Statute, and the court ruled against him, deciding that the First amendment did not apply to his situation.

Brandenburg v. Ohio (1969)

Struck down Ohio's 'criminal anarchy' law

Immigration Act of 1903

Added four classes of people not allowed to immigrate to the US: anarchists, people with epilepsy, beggars, and importers of prostitutes.

United States ex. rel. John Turner v. Williams (1904)

Abraham Isaak et al. believed Czolgosz was a spy
Free Society article outing him

Written by Abraham Isaak: "The attention of comrades is called to another spy. He is well dressed, of medium height, rather narrow shouldered, blond, and about 25 years of age. Up to the present he has made an appearance in Chicago and Cleveland. In the former place he remained but a short time, while in Cleveland he disappeared when the comrades had confirmed themselves of his identity and were on the point of exposing him... If this same individual makes his appearance elsewhere the comrades are warned in advance, and can act accordingly."
